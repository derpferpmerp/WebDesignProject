    Review on the subjects found in the table:


    The Human Development Index is rating based on the statistics of any location.
    The HDI analyzes data from the education level, the life expectancy,
    and the average income per person (PPP/GDP).


    The GDP per Capita statistic refers to the Gross Domestic Output
    divided by the population (amount of economic resources divided by
    the amount of people).


    The Gini Index measures the average wealth the citizens have, and then
    compares them to the amount of people who share the same income rate. For example,
    a very steep graphed Gini Index plot would reveal that the majority of the
    population is poor, and there are one or two people who are billionaires.
